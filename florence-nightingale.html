Florence Nightingale: Deserving Hero

January 11, 2019

There will always come a time when people must be the first to begin creating the change they wish to see. Florence Nightingale taught the people in her world to see it as she did; a world where everyone could access medical assistance regardless of their position in life. Nightingale grew up in a wealthy family. She was expected to grow up, marry, and have children. This was not to be. In 1837, Nightingale felt that she had been sent a mission by God to help others. This led to her resolve to become a nurse, despite the social stigma of the job. Nightingale spent some time touring workhouses, and when she visited one in particular in the 1840s, she resolved to make a difference. Regardless of her parents’ protests, she attended nursing schools and became a superintendent at the Institute for the Care of Sick Gentlewomen upon her return. Later, she was sent to Crimea, where she took charge of the hospitals and proved to the officers that although she was a woman in a man’s world, she could do her job well. Despite falling ill during her time there, she continued her work influencing officials to pass reforms that would benefit the general population. Her hard work made her the first woman to receive the Order of Merit, having been awarded it by King Edward VII. She was also awarded the Royal Red Cross by Queen Victoria. She worked tirelessly, putting aside her own health to help those poorer than her and to remove obstacles concerning health care from their way. Florence Nightingale, a nurse who pioneered numerous health reforms, proved she is a deserving hero because of her selflessness and ambitiousness.

Florence Nightingale’s selflessness was accentuated by her need to fix the problems she perceived in the world she lived in. During the war in Crimea, Nightingale was elected to lead a group of nurses to help the hospital there. While there, she struggled with a rapidly spreading cholera outbreak, but sources said that “Nightingale made it her mission to improve hygiene practices, significantly lowering the death rate at the hospital in the process. The hard work took a toll on her health” (“Florence Nightingale”, History.com). Despite the challenge of aiding sick soldiers and grappling with a sickness that spread too quickly to contain, Nightingale continued to fight. Her selfless nature was a characteristic not customarily seen in her time. While others would have given up and moved on, relieving themselves of unnecessary pressure, she pushed on. As she battled hardship after hardship, her dedication never wavered. Her willingness to ignore her own health indicates her dedication to her mission to help others. Putting others before oneself is a commonly perceived heroic trait as it demonstrates selflessness and willingness to aid others not matter the circumstances, which is a trait that Nightingale certainly possessed. Although she contracted “Crimean Fever”, as a result of which she was bedridden for the rest of her life, she remained: “[…] dedicated as ever to improving health care and alleviating patients’ suffering, [and] continued her work from her bed” (“Florence Nightingale”, Biography.com). Although Nightingale was ill and could not continue her work as a nurse as she had previously, she continued to work from the sidelines. In direct contrast to how many would have responded to being bedridden, she continued working for the good of others rather than herself. Nightingale’s actions demonstrated her strong-willed character, as well as her ability to give to others without looking for repayment as she continued her work, ignoring her own needs and personal struggles. The selflessness Nightingale displayed in the time she lived in and struggles she faced mark her a hero for all.

Florence Nightingale had ambitions that would benefit others and was always determined to succeed. At the time, some doctors and nurses had the idea of perhaps changing how things were run on a smaller scale “but Nightingale's vision [...] was much bolder: that the care given in the workhouse infirmaries should be just as good as that in the best nursed civil hospitals in the suburbs” (McDonald). She moved against society’s standards and pushed towards the reality of equal healthcare for all. The infirmaries were subpar, and often those at workhouses did not have proper care. Nightingale had noticed this during one of her visits and sought to fix the problem. This was not viewed as a problem for many, and others often scorned her attempts to improve healthcare. Nevertheless, she pushed on relentlessly, working to alleviate problems the rest of Britain turned a blind eye on. Others would rather ignore the problems that do not concern them, but Nightingale did not. Her ambitiousness to improve the quality of life others had was something rarely seen in. Her work was not only limited to workhouse infirmaries: “Her statistical research influenced reforms in hospital administration and design […] as well as reforms in hygiene and health care for the poor” (Jenkins). Nightingale was ambitious but focused on helping the poor. Few in Britain at the time were willing to use their own wealth and time to help those considered beneath them. Nightingale, on the other hand, not only used her wealth and time, she threw herself into her work. She used never before seen methods to find what was wrong with hospitals and improve them. Her methods included statistics, a skill that at the time was not considered feminine. Her actions set precedents as she continued to reform a badly damaged system. Although ambitiousness itself is perhaps not heroic, Nightingale used her ambitions to aid others, which is the sign of a true hero.

Nightingale’s fiercely ambitious nature and selfless actions made a strong case in the defense of her heroism. Her pushes for reforms in health care and hospital sanitation showed an ambition that was unheard of for women in her time. She paid attention to matters that were considered insignificant and struggled to show the world that all deserved equal care. Nightingale developed new methods for analysis, an action that many deemed pointless for a woman. However, she, unlike many of the wealthy, worked hard and pushed aside her own desires to aid those who needed it. Her actions are a true inspiration to others, working to show them that change in society is achievable. Despite the tremendous odds she faced, she continued to do what was right in a world where men held all of the power. She demonstrated that women can possess both power and influence if they do not turn back when times are rough. Nightingale utilized the skills she possessed and had gained through personal struggles to help others in ways that could barely be imagined in her time, marking her as a deserving hero.
